📖 Biography

Catherine Laga'aia is a New Zealand-born actress of Samoan descent making her feature film debut as Moana. Born into a creative family with deep Polynesian root

🎬 Career Story

Catherine grew up in a creative household. Her father, Jay Laga'aia, is a well-known New Zealand-Australian actor and singer, famous for roles in Star Wars: Episode II and stage productions like The Lion King. Surrounded by performance and music from a young age, Catherine developed a passion for acting. When Disney announced the live action Moana remake would feature a new young actress as the lead, the search was extensive. Thousands of young Pacific Islander actresses were considered. Catherine was chosen for her natural talent, on-screen charisma, and deep cultural connection to the Polynesian world. Stepping into a role originally voiced by Auli'i Cravalho is no small task, but Catherine has approached it with grace and excitement. She represents a new generation of Pacific Islander talent in mainstream Hollywood.
